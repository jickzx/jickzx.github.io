Jealousy

    April 9, 2026

    In short, who can you trust?

    Semester 2 has been an interesting one. My laziness has more than improved, which is worrying. But I've realised my love for hackathons. So, is this me declining my old self and embracing my new self? And during the break, I've slowly rooted back into my old hobbies, playing the 3DS, Wii and also wanting to buy a Switch 2.
    Why can't I have a balance of both?

    It's January, and the exams have ended. I'm planning on going to the UCL RealTech Hackathon with my old Sixth form friends: Edward Saunders, Nirmal Thomas, William Pimentel. Things don't go well and we couldn't finish anything but I've realised that they don't like that I'm using AI, which makes sense. However, I've become more determined to win a hackathon. As such, I sign up for HackLondon and want to find a team there. Surprisingly, Farhan ends up recruiting me. The story goes that we geek around, win 1st place in the Society track, and all is good! That's what it looks like at least. Unfortunately, time goes on and I realise that some of my friends are jealous of me. What did I win? A Meta Quest 3.
    Jealousy here makes sense.

    Unfortunately, it gets worse. My team ends up becoming a Runner Up for a hackathon, and I talk about it in a group chat. What instantly coems afterwards is my friend saying: "Why didnt you invite me?" That is honestly really sad. I didn't expect a congratulations, but it was even sadder to see that message.
    Now this question hangs above my head: How can people use jealousy as an advantage to success?
